Trust Receipt

Cryptographic proof of every
agent-led transaction

When an AI agent completes a checkout on behalf of a user, Kyance issues a Trust Receipt — a cryptographically signed, tamper-evident credential that serves as dispute-grade evidence of exactly who authorized the purchase, what was verified, and what the trust score was at the moment of authorization.

🔐
SD-JWT Verifiable Credential

Trust Receipts are W3C Verifiable Credential-style artifacts serialized as SD-JWT VCs — the same standard used by digital identity wallets worldwide. Selective disclosure lets merchants share only the claims a verifier needs, preserving customer privacy.

⛓️
Cryptographic Transaction Binding

Each receipt is bound to the specific transaction via a SHA-256 checkout hash — preventing replay attacks and ensuring the receipt cannot be reused across different orders, merchants, or amounts. Anti-replay protection is built in by design.

🏛️
395-Day Dispute-Grade Evidence

Receipts are valid for 395 days — covering the full card-network dispute window. When a customer disputes a charge, the merchant presents the Trust Receipt as signed, timestamped proof of the agent's verified identity and the customer's delegated authorization at the time of purchase.
